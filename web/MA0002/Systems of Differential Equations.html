Linear Systems: Theory

A system of linear Differential Equations:

\[ \begin{align} \frac{dx_1}{dt} & = a_{11}x_1 + a_{12}x_2 \\ \frac{dx_2}{dt} & = a_{21}x_1 + a_{22}x_2 \end{align} \]

Matrix form:

\[ \frac{d\vec{x}}{dt} = A\vec{x} \]

Let \(A\) be a \(2 \times 2\) matrix with two real and distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda_1\)Â and \(\lambda_2\) with corresponding eigenvectors \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\). Then

\[ \vec{x} = c_1e^{\lambda_1t}\vec{u} + c_2e^{\lambda_2t}\vec{v} \]

is the general solution.
