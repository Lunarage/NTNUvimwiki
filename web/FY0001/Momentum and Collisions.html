Collisions

Average force

\[ \overline{\vec{F}} = \frac{\vec{p}' - \vec{p}}{\Delta t} \]

Where \(\vec{p}\) is the initial momentum, and \(\vec{p}'\) is the final momentum.

Elastic collision

A collision in which the kinetic energy before and after is the same is called elastic.

Elastic Collisions On a Straight Line

In an elastic collision of two particles moving along a straight line, the laws of conservation of momentum and energy completely determine the final velocities in terms of the initial velocities.

\[ m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = m_1v_1' + m_2v_2' \]

and

\[ \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2 = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^{\prime 2} + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^{\prime 2} \]

If one of the two particles start at rest, we have

\[ \begin{equation} m_1v_1 = m_1v_1^\prime + m_2v_2^\prime \label{momentum1} \end{equation} \] \[ \begin{equation} m_1(v_1-v_1^\prime) = m_2v_2^\prime \label{momentum2} \end{equation} \]

and

\[ \begin{equation} \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^{2\prime} + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^{\prime 2} \label{energy1} \end{equation} \] \[ \begin{equation} m_1(v_1^2-v_1^{\prime 2}) = m_2v_2^{\prime 2} \label{energy2} \end{equation} \] \[ \begin{equation} m_1(v_1-v_1^\prime)(v_1+v_1^\prime) = m_2v_2^{\prime 2} \label{energy3} \end{equation} \]

Divide \(\eqref{energy3}\) by \(\eqref{momentum2}\)

\[ \begin{equation} v_1 + v_1^\prime = v_2^\prime \label{energymomentum1} \end{equation} \]

Combine \(\eqref{energymomentum1}\) and \(\eqref{momentum2}\)

\[ \begin{equation} m_1(v_1 - v_1^\prime) = m_2(v_1 + v_1^\prime) \label{energymomentum2} \end{equation} \] \[ \begin{equation} v_1^\prime = \frac{m_1-m_2}{m_1+m_2}v_1 \label{energymomentumsolution} \end{equation} \]

Inelastic Collisions On a Straight Line

Totally inelastic collision

If the collision is totally inelastic, so a maximum of kinetic energy is lost, then the conservation of momentum suffices to determine the velocities of both particles after the collision.

\[ v_1' = v_2' = v_{CM} = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1+m_2} \]
