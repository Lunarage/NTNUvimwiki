Momentum and Collisions

TL;DR

Momentum

\[ \vec{p} = m\vec{v} \] \[ \frac{\Delta\vec{p}}{\Delta t} = \vec{F} \] \[ \Sigma \vec{p} = [constant] \] \[ \frac{\Delta \vec{P}}{\Delta t} = \vec{F}_{\mathrm{ext}} \]

Center of Mass

\[ x_{CM} = \frac{\Sigma m_n x_n}{\Sigma m_n} \]

Gravitational potential energy

\[ U = Mgy_{CM} \]

Where \(M\) is the total mass and \(y_{CM}\) is the center of mass along the y-axis.

The Motion of the Center of Mass

\[ \vec{P} = M\vec{v}_{CM} \] \[ \vec{F}_{\mathrm{ext}} = M\vec{a}_{CM} \]

Kinetic energy of a system

\[ K = \Sigma \frac{1}{2}m_n v_n^2 \]

Simply the sum of of the kinetic energy

Collisions

Average force

\[ \bar{\vec{F}} = \frac{\vec{p}' - \vec{p}}{\Delta t} \]

Where \(\vec{p}\) is the initial momentum, and \(\vec{p}'\) is the final momentum.

Elastic collision

A collision in which the kinetic energy before and after is the same is called elastic.

Elastic Collisions On a Straight Line

In an elastic collision of two particles moving along a straight line, the laws of conservation of momentum and energy completely determine the final velocities in terms of the initial velocities.

\[ m_1v_1 + m_2+v_2 = m_1v_1' + m_2+v_2' \]

and

\[ \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2 = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^{2\prime} + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^{2\prime} \]

Inelastic Collisions On a Straight Line

Totally inelastic collision

If the collision is totally inelastic, so a maximum of kinetic energy is lost, then the conservation of momentum suffices to determine the velocities of both particles after the collision.

\[ v_1' = v_2' = v_{CM} = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1+m_2} \]
