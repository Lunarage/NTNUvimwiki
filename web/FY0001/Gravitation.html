Gravitation

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

Law of universal gravitation

\[ F = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \]

Where \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) are the masses of two particles and \(r\) is the distance between them.

Gravitational constant

\[ G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \mathrm{Nm^2kg^{-2}} \]

The Measurement of G

Circular Orbits

Linear velocity in a circular orbit around an object with mass \(M\) at distance \(r\) from the center of that mass.

\[ v^2 = \frac{GM}{r} \]

Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy

\[ U = -\frac{GMm}{r} \]

Total mechanical energy

\[ E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GMm}{r} \]
