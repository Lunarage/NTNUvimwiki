Newton's Laws

Newton's First Law

A body at rest remains at rest, and a body in motion continues to move at constant velocity unless acted upon by a force.

Newton's Second Law

\[ \Sigma F = ma \]

Where F is force, m is the inertial mass and a is the acceleration.

The Combination of Forces

\[ a = \frac{1}{m}\Sigma F \]

Newton's Third Law

Whenever a body exerts a force on another body, the latter exerts a force of equal magnitude and opposite direction on the former.

Kinds of Forces; Weight and Normal Force

Motion With a Constant Force

Friction

\[ f_k = \mu_k N \]

Where \(\mu_k\) is the coefficient of static friction and N is the normal force.

Restoring Force of a Spring

\[ F = -kx \]

Where x is the displacement and k is the spring constant.

Force for Uniform Circular Motion

Centripetal Force

\[ F = ma = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2r \]

Where r is the radius, v is the linear velocity, and \(\omega\) is the angular velocity.
