Work and Energy

TL;DR

Work done by a constant force

\[ W = Fs\cos{\theta} \]

Work done by a variable force

\[ W = \overline{F_x}\Delta x \]

Kinetic energy

\[ K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \]

Work-energy theorem

\[ \Delta K = W \]

Gravitational potential energy

\[ U = mgy \]

Mechanical energy

\[ E = K + U \]

Conservation of mechanical energy

\[ E = K + U = [\text{constant}] \]

Conservative force

The work done by the force is zero for any roundtrip.

Work done by a conservative force:

\[ W = U_1 - U_2 \]

Potential energy of a spring

\[ U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \]

Average power

\[ \overline{P} = \frac{W}{\Delta t} \]

Instantaneous power

\[ P = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{W}{\Delta t} \]

Mechanical power delivered by a force

\[ P = Fv~cos{\theta} \]

Work

\[ W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{\Delta x} \]
